These are excerpts of my journal and photos from my 2017 through hike of the Contiental Divide Trail.

Discipline is a distraction
Do what you feel
Chase things that pay dividends
Act sooner

Animal fear

Unmarked unmapped

Will it go?

I remember the time we went backpacking
I was so anxious for all the things
I thought I had to provide
I wish I had known:
The trail provides.

To anyone with open eyes
Pantry emptied into a sturdy pack
The trail provides.

Against cartoonish villains
Thunderstorms,
Raging rivers,
Vanity

The trail provides
Gummy bears,
Shoes,
Shelter,
Everything

Dandelions remind me of you
Unapologetic insistence
That the world needs more yellow
Forever, regardless

The sound of a rock
At terminal velocity
Passing by your ear:
Flight of a hummingbird
Open car window on highway
Boomerang in flight
Anxious heartbeat

Challenge myself physically and mentally every day. Life can be unappreciated, even hated, without contrast.

Learn new skills and revel in the learning process.

Love others. Share in their excitement without reservation. Give everything.

Avoid complexity and responsibility where possible.

Grow within relationships.

Love work. Do work that's easy to love.

Be a finder, not a seeker.

Revel in creativity, the generation of new things and ideas. Value personal satisfaction over the approval of others.

I lay with my back to the ground on rocks and dead plants itchy like wool. Above bats chirp, flip, and swirl to catch bugs. I think about long walks at dusk with dad, throwing a tennis ball, and watching the bats chase it in dramatic dives. Above the bats, a sheet of smoke scrolls past, subtle texture on the sky, dark as a storm. Bits of ash drift on the breeze. My face is smothered in a wet buff, futile attempt to filter smoke. My stomach cramps and gurgles audibly with some infection.

I feel calm. I feel the beauty of every square inch of this damned planet. I'm home.
